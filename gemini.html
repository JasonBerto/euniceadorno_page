About Eunice Adorno

Eunice Adorno Mexico City (1982)

Eunice Adorno is a Mexican photographer whose work spans from documentary photography to other artistic disciplines. Her interests delve into diverse stories within Mexican communities, landscapes, and regions, particularly those whose ways of life and self-representation are deeply intertwined with the past.

Driven by the often-absent memory of these groups or places regarding their own histories, she actively seeks out archives, images, narratives, and other materials to reconstruct alternative perspectives. Her most recent projects explore decaying landscapes and structures, remnants of industrialization, hydraulic engineering, and the broader social infrastructure's decline in Mexico. Across all her photographic and artistic endeavors, rigorous research is a fundamental component in bringing her projects to fruition.

She has been a recipient of the Young Creators Program of the National Fund for Culture and Arts on three occasions and was part of the National System of Creators from 2019-2023. With the photographic essay "Mujeres Flores" (Flower Women), she received the Fernando Benítez National Culture Award (Mexico) in 2010. In 2011, La Fábrica (Spain) published her book Las Mujeres Flores, and she was selected by the World Press Photo Joop Swart for the Masterclass in the Netherlands. In 2012, she was also nominated by Susan Meiselas for the Rudin Prize for Emerging Photographers for The Norton Museum of Art in West Palm Beach, Fl. Her work has been exhibited in Spain, London, Germany, Brazil, the United States, India, and Italy.

She has been part of the Contemporary Photography Seminar at Centro de la Imagen, and has participated in workshops at Gimnasio de Arte y Cultura, among others. She was part of the Artist Residency Program at the International Studio & Curatorial Program in New York and the "Dos Mares" Latin American Art Residency in Marseille, France.

In 2023, she participated with the Aprdelesp architecture office in the Mexican Pavilion at the 18th International Architecture Exhibition at the Venice Biennale, Italy, with a series of photographs of basketball courts in indigenous communities in Puebla, Veracruz, and Sonora. She also participated in the XX Photography Biennial of Centro de la Imagen.

Through her lens, Eunice Adorno continues to illuminate unseen narratives, inviting viewers to engage deeply with the stories and landscapes she captures.
